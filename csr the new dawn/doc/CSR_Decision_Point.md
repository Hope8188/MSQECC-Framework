# CSR Decision Point

## Trigger Condition

This memo states in advance what happens if the channel search returns only drag or kick families and no conservative orbital kernel.

That is now the current state after the first tested representatives of:

- non-Hermitian local channels
- retarded auxiliary channels
- collective channels

## Current Result

The scan found real momentum drift in all three classes.

It did not find a conservative static radial kernel in any tested representative.

So the decision point is not "no drift exists."

It is:

`no viable galaxy kernel has yet emerged from the tested channel classes`

## What This Means For CSR

If CSR is interpreted narrowly as:

- a direct Lindblad-to-force theory of galaxy dynamics

then the current tested channel families do not support it.

If CSR is to survive, it has to move to one of two narrower possibilities:

1. an emergent metric or stress route, where the channel modifies geometry or effective stress before orbit equations are written
2. a deeper code-to-bath reduction that yields a new non-Hermitian or nonlocal operator class not captured by the first tested families

## What Would Trigger Clause 2 In Full

Clause 2 of the falsifiability memo is triggered in full only if:

- no dimensionally consistent map from the bridge rate to a deterministic orbital kernel can be derived without adding a new empirical scale by hand

The current results tighten Clause 2 materially, but they do not complete it.

Why not:

- the tested channel families are representative, not exhaustive
- the emergent-stress route is still logically open
- a deeper many-body reduction has not yet been completed

## The Practical Decision

The practical decision is immediate anyway.

Do not benchmark drag or kick channels as if they were galaxy laws.

Do not rewrite them as MOND analogues.

Do not claim CSR currently derives rotation curves.

From this point on, CSR only survives as a live programme if the next derivation targets one of these:

- emergent stress/metric response from the code
- a new non-Hermitian reduced operator class with a stationary conservative limit

If neither route closes, Clause 2 will be met and CSR as a galaxy-dynamics theory is over.

## Bottom Line

The channel scan did not kill CSR outright.

It did eliminate the easy survival branch.

CSR now needs a qualitatively different derivation, not another variant of the same drag channel.
