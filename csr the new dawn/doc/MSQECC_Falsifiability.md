# MSQECC Falsifiability

## One-Sentence Criterion

MSQECC, as a QECC-based theory of galaxy dynamics, is falsified if no code-derived matter-coupling channel can produce the required pointer-basis dissipator from the `T^3` stabilizer-measurement structure, or if the positivity-enforced frozen SPARC-trained model fails to achieve positive aggregate `R^2` on a held-out sample of at least 20 non-SPARC galaxies with published baryonic decompositions.

## Why This Is The Right Criterion

The first clause is theoretical.

If the code cannot generate the dissipative matter channel it needs, then the current QECC interpretation fails at the algebraic level.

The second clause is empirical.

If the frozen model cannot generalize positively once the positive-density constraint is enforced, then the galaxy result is not robust enough to support a physical theory claim.

## Current Status Against The Criterion

- The syndrome-channel route is narrowed but not dead: the naive commuting coupling fails, while a noncommuting coupling produces the desired `sigma_z` dephasing channel.
- The positivity-enforced branch is not yet implemented as a full refit, and the current unconstrained external benchmark is mixed rather than decisive.

So the framework is not yet falsified by this criterion.

It is also not yet validated by it.
